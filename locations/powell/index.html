The Powell Healthcare Landscape

Powell's healthcare market includes a wide range of medical practices, from solo practitioners and multi-specialty clinics to large hospital-affiliated groups. Each practice type has distinct billing needs:

  • Solo and small practices: Often lack dedicated billing staff and benefit most from full outsourced billing. These Powell practices typically see the largest percentage improvement in collections when switching to professional billing.
  • Multi-specialty groups: Need a billing partner that can handle diverse coding requirements across different specialties while maintaining consistent quality and reporting.
  • Surgical practices: Require expertise in facility and professional fee billing, surgical coding, and anesthesia billing coordination.
  • Mental health providers: Face unique billing challenges including session-based billing, telehealth coding, and varying payer coverage for behavioral health services in Wyoming.
  • Primary care: Benefit from billing services that optimize preventive care coding, chronic care management billing, and value-based care program participation.

Key Billing Metrics for Powell Practices

When evaluating your current billing performance or comparing billing services, focus on these critical metrics:

  • Clean claim rate: The percentage of claims accepted on first submission. Industry benchmark is 95%+. If your practice is below 90%, a professional billing service can make a significant impact.
  • Days in accounts receivable (AR): How long it takes to collect payment. Best-in-class is under 30 days. Many Powell practices see AR days drop from 45+ to under 30 after outsourcing billing.
  • Denial rate: Percentage of claims denied by payers. Professional billing services typically maintain denial rates under 5%, compared to the 10-20% industry average.
  • Collection rate: The percentage of billed charges actually collected. Top billing services achieve 95%+ adjusted collection rates for their Powell clients.
  • Cost to collect: Total billing costs as a percentage of collections. Outsourced billing typically costs 5-8%, often lower than the true cost of maintaining in-house billing staff.
